Paris

Paris, the capital city of France, is known for its art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. The city is famed for its café culture, and landmarks like the Eiffel Tower, the Gothic Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Louvre Museum, home to the Mona Lisa.

Italy

Italy, a European country with a long Mediterranean coastline, has left a powerful mark on Western culture and cuisine. Its capital, Rome, is home to the Vatican as well as landmark art and ancient ruins. Other major cities include Florence, with Renaissance masterpieces such as Michelangelo’s "David" and Brunelleschi's Duomo; Venice, the city of canals; and Milan, Italy’s fashion capital.

Cayman Islands

The Cayman Islands, a British Overseas Territory in the western Caribbean Sea, is known for its pristine beaches, coral reefs, and clear waters. Grand Cayman, the largest island, is famous for its beach resorts, scuba diving, and snorkeling. Stingray City and the sandbar where stingrays gather are popular attractions.
